Global Tariff Database (Beta Version v_beta1-2024-12)

The Global Tariff Database (GTD) provides bilateral statutory tariff rates for 200 importers and their partners, spanning 34 years (1988–2021). It includes both MFN and preferential tariffs and is based on HS6-level tariff rates (HS88/92 Nomenclature), covering over 6.9 billion observations.

The GTD does not include tariff types beyond MFN and preferential tariffs, such as safeguards, anti-dumping tariffs, or those related to the US trade war.

Find details about the available variables in the GTD and the reference data in the dictionary below. The accompanying paper and its Online Appendix C provide a detailed description of the algorithm.

Global Tariff Database: U.S. Trade War Extension (Version v_2025-10)

Overview

The Global Tariff Database: U.S. Trade War Extension (GTD—U.S. Trade War) provides daily, product-level data on U.S. import and export tariffs vis-à-vis all trading partners from January 2018 through August 15, 2025. This extension builds on the Global Tariff Database (v beta1-2024-12), incorporating all tariff measures imposed by the United States and applied to U.S. exports abroad during the 2018–2025 trade wars, consolidated into a single dataset. For an extensive description of the construction of the GTD—U.S. Trade War, please refer to the accompanying paper and its Online Appendix A.

Download the Data

Tariff data are available at both the national tariff-line and harmonized HS6 (HS 2017) levels in .dta and .csv formats.

If you would like to use the dataset, please cite it as:

"The Global Tariff Database: U.S. Trade War Extension (v_2025-10) from Rodríguez-Clare et al. (2025), based on the methodology developed in Teti (2024)."
